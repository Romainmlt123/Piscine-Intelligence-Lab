# English Grammar Basics

## Present Simple Tense
The present simple is used for habits, general truths, and routines.

**Structure:** Subject + base verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)

**Examples:**
- I work every day.
- She plays tennis on Sundays.
- The sun rises in the east.

**Negative:** Subject + do/does + not + base verb
- I do not (don't) like coffee.
- He does not (doesn't) eat meat.

## Present Continuous Tense
The present continuous is used for actions happening now or temporary situations.

**Structure:** Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

**Examples:**
- I am reading a book right now.
- They are playing football.
- She is studying for her exam.

## Past Simple Tense
The past simple is used for completed actions in the past.

**Structure:** Subject + past form of verb

**Examples:**
- I visited Paris last year.
- She watched a movie yesterday.
- They went to the beach.

**Irregular verbs:** go → went, see → saw, eat → ate, have → had

## Present Perfect Tense
The present perfect connects past actions to the present.

**Structure:** Subject + have/has + past participle

**Examples:**
- I have finished my homework.
- She has lived here for 5 years.
- They have never been to Japan.

**Key words:** already, yet, never, ever, just, for, since

## Vocabulary: Common Phrasal Verbs
- **look up** - to search for information
- **give up** - to stop trying
- **take off** - to remove (clothes) or leave the ground (plane)
- **put on** - to wear
- **turn on/off** - to start/stop a device
- **wake up** - to stop sleeping
- **get up** - to rise from bed
